About Me

I recently graduated from the City University of New York's Queens College with a Bachelors degree in Computer Science. After completing an Associates Degree (Mathematics and Science) at Queensborough Community College, I continued my education at Queens College, where I was a member of the Transfer Honors Program, which recognizes extra-curricular and academic excellence.

I have worked as an cybersecurity research intern at US Department of Energy Pacific Northwest National Laboratory where I contributed to research on the resilience of critical infrastructure systems, creating simplified models of the power grid. Specifically, I investigated the viability of the use of various procedural generation algorithms in their use in generating models of the power grid.

I am passionate about introducing coding to others; as a student at QCC; I was an instructor for the NYS Science and Technology Entry Program (STEP). In this role, I developed and taught a curriculum to underrepresented and/or disadvantaged middle and high school students on robotics, using Scratch and Python.

I was recipient of the CUNY Research Scholars Program scholarship, where under the mentorship of Dr. Yusuf Dansiman, I analyzed and optimized different cross validation techniques in their use in time-series datasets.
